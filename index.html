Jamie Lee

Graduate Student | User Researcher

Exploring the nexus between qualitative and quantitative research and analysis, in order to better understand how the presentation of information influences decision-making and how technology can affirm and challenge existing political, cultural, and social beliefs.

Research Highlights

Affinity diagram to organize qualitative findings, gathered through contextual inquiry

Qualitative coding of user interactions with YouTube Music, gathered from usability tests

Data visualizations of user behavior and satisfaction levels, based on quantitative findings gathered through user surveys

ANOVA and post-hoc statistical tests to evaluate significant differences between student groups

Interaction map of YouTube Music, used to better understand user journeys and identify potential pain points

Heuristic evaluation of Levi's mobile application to improve content placement

Behavioral analytics on users visiting The Wharton School's undergraduate website

Persona development, based on observations gathered through user interviews

Chi-square tests to evaluate relationship between university student groups and financial concerns
